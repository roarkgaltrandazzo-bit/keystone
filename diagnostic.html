What It Is

A short engagement with a clear answer.

The diagnostic combines owner interviews, review of current sales and pricing materials, pipeline visibility, account structure, role clarity, and the decisions that still run through the owner. The goal is not to bury you in a report. The goal is to identify the few commercial gaps that are creating the most drag.

You leave with a practical read on the current state of the business, a prioritized path forward, and a decision about whether Keystone should help build the next stage.

What Gets Assessed

Six areas that usually decide whether growth is transferable.

Sales process

How opportunities are qualified, advanced, followed up, and closed.

Pricing

How pricing decisions are made, approved, protected, and reviewed.

Account management

How key relationships are owned, documented, transferred, and developed.

Pipeline

Whether leadership has a real read on what is moving, stuck, or at risk.

Roles

Who owns selling, approving, reviewing, escalating, and following through.

Owner dependency

Where the owner is still the system, even if the company has people in place.

Deliverables

What you walk away with.

Gap Analysis
A plain-English review of where the commercial system is holding, where it is exposed, and where owner dependency is still creating risk.
Prioritized Path Forward
A ranked set of changes, based on impact and practicality, so the next move is clear instead of theoretical.
Honest Read
A direct recommendation on whether Keystone should help with Stage 2, whether your team can fix it internally, or whether the timing is wrong.

Pricing

Pricing is confirmed before the diagnostic starts.

The diagnostic is scoped after the first conversation based on company size, number of stakeholders, and the complexity of the commercial function.

No surprise scope. You will know the fee, timeline, and deliverables before anything starts. If the diagnostic is not the right fit, that will be clear before you spend money.

After The Diagnostic

Two paths. No pressure theater.

If the diagnostic shows Keystone can help, the next step is Stage 2: Architecture Design. That is where the blueprint gets built: customer focus, qualification logic, pricing decision rights, account structure, role clarity, and review cadence.

If the fit is wrong, the timing is wrong, or the work should be handled internally, you will hear that plainly. The diagnostic should still leave you with a better read on the business than you had going in.

Fit

Who this is for and who it is not for.

Good fit

  • Owner-led companies with real revenue and informal commercial systems.
  • Businesses where pricing, sales judgment, and key accounts still rely heavily on the owner.
  • Companies preparing to hire, transition leadership, reduce owner dependency, or improve enterprise value.

Bad fit

  • Companies looking for a quick sales script or motivational coaching.
  • Owners who want a tool before fixing the operating logic underneath it.
  • Businesses unwilling to involve leadership in the diagnostic process.
