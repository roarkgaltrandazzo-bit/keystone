The Keystone System

Four stages. One outcome: a commercial system your team can run.

Each stage builds on the last. The point is to turn commercial judgment into operating structure, not to create another planning document nobody uses.

Start With The DiagnosticWho This Helps

Sequence

Stage 1 is the only entry point.

Keystone does not start by prescribing a CRM, sales hire, pricing tool, or leadership cadence. We start by understanding where the commercial system is exposed.

Stage 01
Commercial Diagnostic
Review sales process, pricing, account management, pipeline, roles, and owner dependency. The deliverable is a clear gap analysis and a prioritized path forward.
2 to 3 weeks | Entry point
Stage 02
Architecture Design
Build the blueprint: customer focus, qualification logic, pricing decision rights, account structure, role clarity, and review cadence.
4 to 6 weeks | Blueprint
Stage 03
System Build
Install the roles, tools, rhythms, scorecards, templates, and accountability structures with your team. Built to transfer, not to create consultant dependency.
60 to 90 days | Build and transfer
Stage 04
Ongoing Advisory
Quarterly reviews, optimization, and commercial guidance once the system is installed. Useful for owners who want a steady outside read.
Quarterly | Optional

What Gets Built

Practical structure your team can operate.

Customer Focus
Clear definition of which customers are worth pursuing, protecting, expanding, or walking away from.
Qualification Logic
A shared standard for what deserves sales attention, what needs more information, and what should not enter the pipeline.
Pricing Decision Rights
Approval levels, exception rules, margin logic, and escalation paths so pricing does not depend on whoever feels pressure first.
Account Structure
Key account ownership, review cadence, relationship mapping, and growth priorities that are not trapped in the owner's head.
Commercial Cadence
Pipeline, pricing, and account review rhythms that help leaders make decisions instead of reciting activity updates.
