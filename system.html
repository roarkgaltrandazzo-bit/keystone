How Keystone Works

Diagnosis first. Build second.

The work starts by understanding how revenue is currently produced, priced, tracked, managed, and protected. The build comes after the commercial gaps are clear.

Sequence

Four stages, built to transfer.

Stage 01
Revenue System Review
Review sales process, pricing, customer mix, CRM usage, pipeline reliability, roles, and commercial dependency. The deliverable is a findings report and prioritized fix list.
2 to 3 weeks | Entry point
Stage 02
Architecture Design
Build the blueprint: customer focus, qualification logic, pricing decision rights, account structure, role clarity, and review cadence.
4 to 6 weeks | Blueprint
Stage 03
System Build
Install the roles, tools, rhythms, scorecards, templates, and accountability structures alongside your team.
60 to 90 days | Build and transfer
Stage 04
Ongoing Advisory
Quarterly reviews, optimization, and commercial guidance once the system is installed.
Quarterly | Optional

What Gets Built

Practical structure your team can operate.

Customer Focus
Clear definition of which customers are worth pursuing, protecting, expanding, or walking away from.
Qualification Logic
A shared standard for what deserves sales attention, what needs more information, and what should not enter the pipeline.
Pricing Decision Rights
Approval levels, exception rules, margin logic, and escalation paths.
Account Structure
Key account ownership, review cadence, relationship mapping, and growth priorities.
Commercial Cadence
Pipeline, pricing, and account review rhythms that help leaders make decisions.
