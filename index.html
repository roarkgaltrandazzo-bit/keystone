FAQ

Questions buyers usually ask before the first conversation.

How is this different from a business coach?

Keystone is not built around motivation, accountability calls, or general advice. The work is focused on commercial structure: sales process, pricing logic, customer focus, CRM habits, pipeline rhythm, and role clarity.

How is this different from a fractional sales leader?

A fractional sales leader usually steps into the function. Keystone helps define and build the function, especially when the role, process, tools, and management rhythm are not ready yet.

What happens during a Revenue System Review?

We review how revenue moves through the business: leads, quotes, follow-up, pipeline, pricing, CRM usage, customer mix, sales roles, and owner dependency. The output is a written findings report and a prioritized fix list.

Do we need a CRM before this starts?

No. A CRM can help, but it will not fix unclear stages, weak follow-up standards, bad data, loose pricing, or an undefined sales role.

How long does the review take?

Most reviews take two to three weeks, depending on company size, number of stakeholders, and how much existing information is available.

Is this for owners, investors, or both?

Both. Owners usually want better control over revenue. Investors usually want to understand how revenue is produced, protected, and improved before or after a transaction.
